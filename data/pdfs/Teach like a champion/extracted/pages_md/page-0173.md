# Primary-Level Knowledge Organizers

## Year One Science: Seasons and the Weather

### "Foul Weather"

| Term      | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Flood     | So much rain that rivers overflow their banks. |
| Hurricane | Huge swirling storm with heavy rains and dangerous winds. Comes off the water. Take cover! |
| Blizzard  | A big snow storm. Often windy and hard to see. |
| Tornado   | A small but dangerous swirling cloud. Formed on land mostly. Take cover! |

### Measuring Weather

| Term          | Description |
|---------------|-------------|
| Data          | A collection of facts, often numbers, that can tell us important things |
| Rain Gauge    | A tool for measuring how much rain has fallen |
| Weather Vane  | A tool that measures the direction and speed of wind |
| Thermometer   | A tool that measures how warm or cold it is |

### Types of Clouds

#### Cirrus Cloud
Thin and delicate, high in the sky, "wispy" like feathers

#### Cumulus Cloud
Puffy or fluffy, like cotton balls, sometimes piled up high.

#### Stratus Cloud
"Blanket clouds," low, thick without a shape.

### The Seasons

| Season | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Spring | After Winter. Plants begin growing. Temperatures warm. Animals come out of hibernation. |
| Summer | After Spring. The warmest season. Days are longest and the sun rises early and sets late. |
| Autumn | After Summer. Temperatures cool and days get shorter. Leaves fall. Crops are harvested. |
| Winter | After Autumn. The coldest season. Short days. Trees are often bare. Some animals hibernate. |

## Ghana and the Ashanti

| Term    | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Ghana   | A country in West Africa with grasslands and rich forests. |
| Ashanti | A group of people who have lived in Ghana for over 400 years. Storytelling and art are important in their culture. |
