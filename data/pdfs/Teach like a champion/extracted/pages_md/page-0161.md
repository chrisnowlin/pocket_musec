## Poetic and Literary Terms

| Term        | Definition                                                                 | Example                                      |
|-------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------|
| Enjambment  | The running-over of a sentence or phrase from one poetic line to the next, without end punctuation |                                              |
| End-Stopped | A poetic line ending with punctuation to show the completion of a phrase   | Uhhh, my mother says.                      |
| Anaphora    | The repetition of a word or phrase at the beginning of lines or stanzas    | Maybe the car [...]<br>Maybe right before [...] |
| Caesura     | A pause within a line of poetry, usually marked by punctuation             | can grow up free. Can grow up                |
| Haiku       | A Japanese poetic form; three unrhymed lines of 5, 7, and 5 syllables      | Even the silence<br>has a story to tell you.<br>Just listen. Listen. |
