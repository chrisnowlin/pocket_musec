8  Tree-ear squeezed the bulging pouch that he wore at his waist.  
9  He had meant to hold back the good news, but the excitement  
10 spilled out of him. “Crane-man! A good thing that you greeted  
11 me so just now, for later today we will have to use the proper  
12 words!” He held the bag high. Tree-ear was delighted when  
13 Crane-man's eyes widened in surprise. He knew that Crane-man  
14 would guess at once—only one thing could give a bag that kind  
15 of smooth fullness. Not carrot-tops or chicken bones,  
16 which protruded in odd lumps. No, the bag was filled with rice.
