# Case Study 1

*Ms. Klein:* What's the definition of *vengeance*? Carla.  
*Carla:* Um ...  
*Ms. Klein:* Shakani? *Vengeance*?  
*Shakani:* *Vengeance* is violent revenge, getting back at someone who got you.  
*Ms. Klein:* Thanks, Shakani. So what's *vengeance*, Carla?  
*Carla:* Violent revenge.  
*Ms. Klein:* So who seeks *vengeance* in *Romeo and Juliet*, Carla?  
*Carla:* Tybalt, when he says, “This must be a Montague. Fetch me my sword.”  
*Ms. Klein:* Tybalt is doing what when he says that?  
*Carla:* Looking for *vengeance*.  
*Ms. Klein:* And would you agree that examples of people seeking *vengeance* are rare indeed in the play?  
*Carla:* No. Pretty much everyone is seeking *vengeance*.  
*Ms. Klein:* Nice, Carla. It does seem like just about everybody had *vengeance* on the brain.
